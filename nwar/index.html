How nWAR is calculated

Netball Wins Above Replacement (nWAR) estimates how many additional wins each player contributes compared to a replacement-level player — roughly the average performance of the bottom 15% of qualified players in the dataset.

The metric uses Champion Data Net Points as its primary currency. Net Points reward positive actions (intercepts, gains, goal assists, deflections, rebounds) and penalise negative ones (turnovers, missed shots, penalties).

Formula

  1. Replacement level — the mean average net points per game of the bottom 15% of qualified players meeting the minimum-games threshold.
  2. NPAR/Game (Net Points Above Replacement per game) — the player's average net points per game minus the replacement level.
  3. nWAR = NPAR/Game × Games played ÷ 16 (the estimated net-point cost of one additional win).

Without position data in the archive, no positional adjustment is applied. Shooters will naturally outscore defenders in net points. Treat nWAR as a general contribution ranking rather than a direct cross-position comparison. A full positional adjustment would require per-match position data to be added to the archive.
