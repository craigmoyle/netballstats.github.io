7 Apr 2026 · Analytical stats first release

Players and teams now have an explainable analytical layer.

The archive gained first-release analytical metrics, formula notes, and player dossier callouts without turning the product into a black-box dashboard.

  • Added analytical archive endpoints and metadata so player and team leaderboards can surface analytical metrics alongside raw totals.
  • Tightened analytical ordering, rounding, and tie-break behaviour so the new metrics read like the rest of the archive rather than as a separate system.
  • Restored contextual cues such as squad colours on analytical season-series output so trend views stay legible and branded.
  • Added grouped analytical controls on the archive, including one-line explainers and average-only locking where totals would be misleading.
  • Linked analytical stat notes to their controls for better screen-reader context and cleaner explanatory UX.
  • Added a player analytical dossier to profile pages and fixed the underlying query scoping so player-specific summaries still use the correct team context.
  • Documented the analytical layer, fixed stat-alias parsing across all supported query patterns, and broadened the nWAR high-`min_games` regression path needed for the final validation pass.

Where to see it

The analytical layer is live on the archive and player dossier surfaces. It does not yet extend to Ask the Stats, round recap, or a separate analytics landing page.

  • Reader impact Explainable formulas, analytical rankings, and player-level analytical notes.
  • Touches Archive leaderboards, season series, player profiles, docs, and regression coverage.

7 Apr 2026 · Editorial almanac refresh

The archive and player profiles now read more like a sports almanac than a dashboard.

A full UX pass reshaped the archive shell, the leaderboard reading order, and the player profile into a more editorial, more legible reference surface.

  • Added the new editorial almanac layout shells that now frame the archive and player views.
  • Reworked the archive homepage into a clearer control desk with a reading guide so the filtering flow feels deliberate rather than dashboard-like.
  • Expanded the player leaderboard and added dossier cues so the archive points readers toward the deeper player profile surfaces.
  • Turned the player profile into an editorial dossier with stronger hierarchy, context blocks, and season-ledger framing.
  • Kept the season ledger notes in sync with total versus average toggles so the copy tracks the mode the table is actually showing.
  • Strengthened the verifier and landed a final polish pass to tighten the overall editorial system.

What changed in feel

The archive still does the same job, but the reading order is sharper: scan, narrow, compare, then open the dossier.

  • Reader impact Cleaner hierarchy, better wayfinding, and more distinctive page identities.
  • Touches Homepage shell, leaderboard context, player profile structure, and supporting QA.

7 Apr 2026 · Historical coverage and data corrections

Older ANZ Championship seasons now read more honestly and more cleanly.

Recent fixes corrected participation counting, clarified the 2008 archive boundary, and made the season tables easier to scan.

  • Lowered the archive season filter floor from 2017 to 2008 so older ANZ Championship coverage can be queried directly.
  • Added a `gamesPlayed` stat and corrected match counting so the archive tracks actual played appearances rather than loose roster presence.
  • Shifted player-profile games played to a participation-based filter (`>= 1` minute) to remove inflated totals from non-appearances.
  • Updated the `superNetballR` source package to v0.3.1 and then fixed ANZ Championship 2008–2014 participation coverage in `player_match_participation`.
  • Clarified the public copy so the archive explicitly says 2008 finals only, with full-season coverage beginning in 2009.
  • Improved season-ledger readability with rotated abbreviations, a vertical Games header, and left-aligned table headers.

Why it matters

These fixes make player histories and older-season browsing line up more closely with what supporters expect from the historical record.

  • Reader impact More trustworthy games-played totals and clearer older-season coverage.
  • Touches Archive filtering, player participation logic, source data, and season-table presentation.
